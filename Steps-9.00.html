Transforming the Data in Power Query Editor 9
1. Click on the drop down button in Year Column and Select Years from 2001 to 2019, and Entity column drop down and unselect 'World', those are the years we are going to analyze. 

2. Rename Entity column to Country
3. Rename the table 'Fuel Production and Consumption' to 'Fuel'

4.Select Columns Coal Production per capita, Coal Consumption per capita, Gas Production per capita, Gas Consumption per capita, Oil Production per Capita, Oil Consumption per Capita
5. Right Click on any selected column title.
6. Click Remove Columns

7. Data Conversion
i) Select Add Custom Column from Add Column Ribbon and use the formula below to convert Gas Production metrics from cubic meter (m3) to kwh and rename the custom column as Gas Production (kwh)
Gas:
1 m3 = 11.363 kwh
https://myboiler.com/gas-volume-m3-to-kwh-calculator/

ii) Do the above step for Gas Consumption as well.
iii) Repeat the same step for Converting Coal Production and Coal Consumption to convert metrics from Ton to kwh using the below formula
Coal:
1 Ton = 8141 kwh
https://www.convertunits.com/from/tonne+of+coal+equivalent/to/kWh
iv) Repeat the above step for Oil Production and Oil Consumption to covert metrics from cubic meters to kwh for oil using the below formula.
Oil:
1 m3 = 10241.378  kwh
https://www.unitconverters.net/volume/cubic-meter-to-barrel-oil.htm
https://www.unitjuggler.com/convert-energy-from-boe-to-kWh.html

v)Change all these custom columns datatype to Decimal Number
8. Choose the Coal Production(ton) ,Coal consumption (ton), Gas Production (m3), Gas Consumption (m3), Oil Production (m3), Oil Consumption(m3)  and Right click and Remove these Columns
9. Add 2 custom columns for Total Fuel Production and Consumptions using the below formula and data type as Decimal Number.
Total Fuel Production = Coal Production(kwh)+Gas Production(kwh)+Oil Production(kwh)
Total Fuel Consumption = Coal Consumption(kwh)+Gas Consumption(kwh)+Oil Consumption(kwh)


10. Now to change ‘year’ column to date format, first Duplicate the Year Column and rename it to Year Source,
11.Then change Original year column data type to text. To do that , in the home ribbon, under transform section there is datatype button and click it and select text.
12. Sort the year column to ascending again (by clicking arrow next to column name and select ascending) , now in the transform ribbon click datatype and select ‘date’.
13. Now the year is in date format, change that column name to ‘Date’.
14. Right Click on the Date Column and Transform-> Year->End of the Year.
15.Now the data in this table is ready to load, click on file and select close & apply. You will see that table loaded into power bi.
16. Repeat the steps 2-4 from (Step 1 Importing Data) to import Country_Continent dataset Excel file.
17. From power query editor, select Country, continent and subregion columns by pressing Ctrl key and Right click and click Remove Other Columns
18. Select Merge Queries from Home in the ribbon and Choose Continent_Country table and Fuel Table and select Country columns from both the tables and Choose Left Outer Join. Click Ok.
19. Fuel Table will be added with a new column-> Expand that column by clicking on Expand button and Select Continent and Country to Expand and hit ok.
20. Rename Both the columns as Country_New and Continent. Right click each column and unselect Null values from both the columns. Then Click Remove Empty from Continent column. Click Close and Apply.
