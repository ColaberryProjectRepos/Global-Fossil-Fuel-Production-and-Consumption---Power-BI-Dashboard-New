According to data from the International Energy Agency (IEA), fossil fuels accounted for approximately 77% of China's total primary energy  consumption in 2019 (Coal - 58% , Oil - 11%, Natural Gas - 5%, Other Fossil Fuels - 2%) and 81% of the United States' total primary energy consumption in 2019 (Petroleum -37%, Natural Gas -32%, Coal -11%, Nuclear energy - 8%, Renewable energy - 11%).
While this represents a decrease from previous years, China and United States remain heavily dependent on fossil fuels to meet its energy needs.

Therefore to reduce fossil fuel consumption by major consumers , I recommend 
  • Transitioning to renewable energy sources: Encourage the development and use of renewable energy sources, such as solar, wind, hydroelectric, nuclear fusion and geothermal energy, to reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
  • Improving energy efficiency: Encourage the adoption of energy-efficient technologies and practices, such as LED lighting, insulation, and efficient heating and cooling systems, to reduce energy consumption.
  • Investing in public transportation: Public transportation, such as buses and trains, help reduce the number of cars on the road and the amount of fuel consumed by individuals.
  • Encouraging telecommute: Encourage telecommuting and remote work to reduce the amount of transportation required for commuting.
  • Encouraging the use of electric vehicles: Encourage the use of electric vehicles by providing incentives such as tax credits and installing more charging stations.
  • Encouraging community-based energy projects: Encourage the development of community-based energy projects, such as community solar and wind projects, which can reduce the reliance on fossil fuels and create local economic benefits.
  • Supporting small-scale renewable energy projects: Support small-scale renewable energy projects, such as rooftop solar panels and small wind turbines, to increase the use of distributed renewable energy sources.
  • Promoting eco-tourism: Promote eco-tourism and sustainable travel practices to reduce the carbon footprint of the travel industry.
