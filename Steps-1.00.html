Problem Statement 1

World's dependence on fossil fuels is contributing to the acceleration of climate change, rising global temperatures, sea level rise, ocean acidification and environmental degradation. The challenge is to reduce global dependence on fossil fuels, transition to cleaner and renewable energy sources, and address the social, economic, and environmental consequences of the fossil fuel industry.

Global fossil fuel production increased 59% in 2019 when compared to 2001 and global fossil fuel consumption increased 35% in 2019 when compared to 2001.
The United States was the largest producer of fossil fuels in 2019, producing about 20% of the world's total fossil fuel production, followed by Russia.
China and United States are the largest consumer of fossil fuels in the world, accounting for nearly 28% (China) and 15% (United States) of global consumption in 2019.
